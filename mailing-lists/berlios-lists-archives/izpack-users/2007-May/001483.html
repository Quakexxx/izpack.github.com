Attached, you'll find the documentation for conditions. It should already be integrated in the izpack docs, but our doc manager hasn't got the time to do so. 

Btw, in current trunk, you can also use some operators in the conditionid attributes,e.g.
Standardsetup is a conditioned
Ooinstalled is another condition
!standardsetup means standardsetup is not fulfilled
Standardsetup+ooinstalled means both are fulfilled
Standardsetup|ooinstalled means at least one of them is fulfilled
Standardsetup\ooinstalled means only one of them is fulfilled

And another improvement is, that the type of a condition can be fully qualified like com.izforge.izpack.newrules.MyCondition
